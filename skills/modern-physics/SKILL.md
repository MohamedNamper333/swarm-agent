# Modern Physics

Advanced specialist in 20th/21st-century physics covering quantum theory, relativity, particle physics, nuclear structure, and cosmology. Emphasizes mathematical rigor, conceptual clarity through thought experiments, and the symmetry-driven structure of fundamental physics.

## When to Use This Skill

- Solving non-relativistic quantum mechanics problems: wavefunctions, potentials, angular momentum, spin
- Applying special relativity: Lorentz transformations, time dilation, length contraction, spacetime diagrams, energy-momentum relations
- Working with general relativity: metric tensors, geodesics, Schwarzschild solution, gravitational waves
- Analyzing particle physics using the Standard Model: quarks, leptons, gauge bosons, Feynman diagrams
- Computing nuclear binding energies, decay modes, fission/fusion reactions, and cross-sections
- Exploring cosmological models: Friedmann equations, Lambda-CDM, inflation, Big Bang nucleosynthesis, dark energy

## Key Capabilities

- Solve the Schrödinger equation for 1D/3D potentials (infinite well, harmonic oscillator, hydrogen atom) using analytical and approximation methods
- Apply Lorentz transformations and four-vector formalism to relativistic kinematics and dynamics
- Derive predictions from the Friedmann equations for expanding universes with various matter/energy compositions
- Construct and interpret Feynman diagrams for QED and QCD scattering processes at tree level
- Compute decay rates, half-lives, and Q-values for alpha, beta, and gamma nuclear decay processes
- Reason through quantum paradoxes (EPR, Schrödinger's cat, measurement problem) using formal quantum postulates
- Use Noether's theorem to connect symmetries with conservation laws across physical theories

## Core Concepts

### Quantum Mechanics
- Wave-particle duality; de Broglie wavelength; complementarity
- Schrödinger equation (time-dependent and time-independent); Born probability rule
- Hilbert space formalism; operators; observables; commutators; uncertainty principle
- Infinite/finite square wells; harmonic oscillator; hydrogen atom; periodic potentials
- Angular momentum: orbital and spin; Pauli matrices; addition of angular momentum
- Perturbation theory (time-independent and time-dependent); Fermi's golden rule
- Entanglement; Bell's theorem; EPR paradox; quantum teleportation; density matrices

### Special Relativity
- Lorentz transformations; spacetime interval; light cones; worldlines
- Time dilation, length contraction, and relativity of simultaneity
- Relativistic energy and momentum: E = mc², energy-momentum relation for massless and massive particles
- Four-vector formalism; Lorentz scalars; proper time
- Relativistic Doppler effect; velocity addition formula
- Relativistic kinematics of particle decays and collisions

### General Relativity
- Equivalence principle; gravitational redshift; geodesic equation
- Metric tensor; Christoffel symbols; Riemann curvature tensor
- Einstein field equations (vacuum and with matter/energy)
- Schwarzschild solution; event horizon; black holes; singularity theorems
- Gravitational waves: linearized gravity, quadrupole formula, LIGO detection
- Cosmology: FLRW metric, Friedmann equations, Hubble's law, cosmological constant

### Particle Physics and the Standard Model
- Fundamental particles: quarks (up, down, charm, strange, top, bottom), leptons (e, mu, tau, neutrinos)
- Gauge bosons: photon (QED), W/Z (weak), gluons (QCD), Higgs boson
- Strong interaction: color charge, confinement, asymptotic freedom, parton model
- Weak interaction: V-A structure, parity violation, CKM matrix, neutrino oscillations
- Spontaneous symmetry breaking and the Higgs mechanism
- Feynman diagrams: vertices, propagators, crossing symmetry, Mandelstam variables

### Nuclear Physics
- Nuclear binding energy and liquid drop model; semi-empirical mass formula
- Radioactive decay: alpha, beta (plus electron capture), gamma; decay chains
- Nuclear reactions: Q-value, cross-section, compound nucleus, direct reactions
- Fission: chain reaction, critical mass, reactor physics
- Fusion: Coulomb barrier, cross-section, stellar nucleosynthesis, tokamaks

### Cosmology
- Big Bang theory; Hubble expansion; cosmic microwave background
- Primordial nucleosynthesis: light element abundances
- Dark matter: evidence (rotation curves, gravitational lensing, CMB), candidates (WIMPs, axions)
- Dark energy: cosmological constant, accelerated expansion, equation of state
- Cosmic inflation: horizon and flatness problems, quantum fluctuations, primordial power spectrum
- Structure formation: Jeans instability, growth of perturbations, large-scale structure

## Best Practices

- Distinguish quantum regimes by comparing length/time scales to characteristic de Broglie wavelength and Compton wavelength
- Always verify Lorentz invariance of computed quantities in relativistic problems
- Use natural units (ℏ = c = 1) when doing particle physics calculations, restoring physical units only at the final step
