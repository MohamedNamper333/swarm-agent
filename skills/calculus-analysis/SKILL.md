# Calculus & Analysis

Rigorous specialist in calculus and mathematical analysis covering differential/integral calculus in single and multiple variables, real and complex analysis, vector calculus, and advanced problem-solving with delta-epsilon methods and proof strategies.

## When to Use This Skill

- Computing limits, derivatives, integrals, and infinite series for closed-form and numerical problems
- Proving continuity, differentiability, integrability, and convergence using epsilon-delta arguments
- Optimizing functions of one or many variables with constraints using Lagrange multipliers
- Solving problems in complex analysis: contour integration, residue theorem, conformal mapping
- Applying vector calculus: gradient, divergence, curl; Green's, Stokes', and divergence theorems
- Analyzing sequences and series for convergence, uniform convergence, and power series expansions

## Key Capabilities

- Compute derivatives using limit definition, differentiation rules, implicit differentiation, and logarithmic differentiation
- Evaluate definite and indefinite integrals using substitution, integration by parts, partial fractions, trigonometric substitution, and numerical methods
- Prove convergence of sequences and series using comparison, ratio, root, integral, and alternating series tests
- Perform contour integration in the complex plane using Cauchy's integral formula, residue theorem, and Jordan's lemma
- Derive and apply the fundamental theorems of vector calculus: gradient theorem, Green's theorem, Stokes' theorem, divergence theorem
- Solve optimization problems with and without constraints using critical points, Hessian analysis, and Lagrange multipliers
- Construct rigorous delta-epsilon proofs for limits, continuity, and uniform continuity

## Core Concepts

### Differential Calculus
- Formal definition of limit; delta-epsilon proofs; one-sided and infinite limits
- Continuity: definition, types of discontinuities, intermediate value theorem, extreme value theorem
- Derivative: definition as limit of difference quotient; tangent line interpretation
- Differentiation rules: product, quotient, chain rule; implicit differentiation
- Mean value theorem (Rolle, Lagrange, Cauchy); L'Hôpital's rule; Taylor's theorem
- Multivariable: partial derivatives, gradient, directional derivative, Jacobian, Hessian
- Optimization: critical points, second derivative test, Lagrange multipliers, convexity

### Integral Calculus
- Riemann integral: partitions, upper/lower sums, integrability criteria
- Fundamental theorem of calculus (parts 1 and 2); antiderivatives
- Integration techniques: substitution, integration by parts, trigonometric integrals/substitutions, partial fractions
- Improper integrals: unbounded intervals and integrands; comparison tests
- Multivariable: double/triple integrals, Fubini's theorem, change of variables, Jacobian
- Line integrals: scalar and vector fields; path independence; conservative fields
- Applications: area, volume, arc length, surface area, center of mass, work, flux

### Real Analysis
- Real number system: completeness axiom, supremum/infimum, Archimedean property
- Sequences: convergence, monotone convergence theorem, Bolzano-Weierstrass, Cauchy sequences
- Series: convergence tests, absolute vs conditional convergence, rearrangement theorems
- Continuity: epsilon-delta, uniform continuity, Lipschitz continuity, function composition
- Differentiability: Darboux's theorem, chain rule proof, inverse function theorem
- Riemann-Stieltjes integral; integration of continuous and monotone functions
- Sequences and series of functions: pointwise/uniform convergence, Weierstrass M-test, power series, Stone-Weierstrass

### Complex Analysis
- Complex numbers: algebra, polar form, roots of unity, regions in complex plane
- Analytic functions: Cauchy-Riemann equations, harmonic functions, entire functions
- Complex integration: Cauchy's theorem, Cauchy's integral formula, derivatives of analytic functions
- Series: power series, Taylor series, Laurent series, radius of convergence
- Singularities: removable, poles, essential; classification and residues
- Residue theorem; evaluation of real integrals using contour integration
- Conformal mapping: Möbius transformations, Schwarz-Christoffel, applications to potential theory

### Vector Calculus
- Vector fields: gradient, divergence, curl; physical interpretation (flow, flux, circulation)
- Line integrals: work, circulation; path independence; conservative fields and potential functions
- Surface integrals: flux integrals; parameterized surfaces
- Green's theorem in the plane; divergence theorem in the plane
- Stokes' theorem: relationship between circulation and curl
- Divergence theorem (Gauss): relationship between flux and divergence
- Applications: Maxwell's equations, fluid flow, heat conduction, electrostatics

## Best Practices

- Always check conditions of a theorem before applying it (e.g., continuity for MVT, analyticity for Cauchy's theorem)
- When stuck on an inequality, start from the desired conclusion and work backwards with triangular or reverse-triangle inequalities
- Verify solutions to integration problems by differentiating the result
