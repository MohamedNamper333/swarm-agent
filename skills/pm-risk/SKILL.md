# Risk Management

## Risk Management Process
1. **Identify**: find all potential risks (brainstorm, checklists, expert input)
2. **Assess**: probability × impact = risk score
3. **Plan**: decide response strategy
4. **Monitor**: track and review regularly

## Risk Assessment Matrix
| Impact↓ / Probability→ | Low | Medium | High |
|------------------------|-----|--------|------|
| **High** | Medium | High | Critical |
| **Medium** | Low | Medium | High |
| **Low** | Low | Low | Medium |

## Response Strategies
- **Avoid**: eliminate the risk entirely (change approach)
- **Mitigate**: reduce probability or impact
- **Transfer**: shift to third party (insurance, contract, outsourcing)
- **Accept**: acknowledge and budget for contingency
- **Escalate**: push to higher management (if beyond project scope)

## Types of Risk
- **Technical**: architecture, performance, security, scalability
- **Schedule**: timeline, dependencies, sequencing, critical path
- **Resource**: budget, staffing, skill gaps, turnover
- **External**: regulatory, market, vendor, geopolitical
- **Requirements**: unclear, changing, conflicting, unrealistic

## Contingency Planning
- 10-20% management reserve in budget/schedule
- Trigger conditions: what events activate contingency?
- Fallback plans: what if primary mitigation fails?
- Pre-mortem: assume failure, work backwards to causes
