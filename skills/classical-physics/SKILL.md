# Classical Physics

Deep specialist in classical physics spanning Newtonian mechanics, thermodynamics, wave phenomena, acoustics, fluid dynamics, and Maxwellian electromagnetism. Focuses on quantitative problem-solving, dimensional analysis, and connecting physical principles to engineering and natural phenomena.

## When to Use This Skill

- Solving kinematics, dynamics, or statics problems involving forces, energy, momentum, and rigid-body motion
- Applying thermodynamic laws to heat engines, entropy calculations, and phase transitions
- Analyzing wave propagation, interference, diffraction, and optical systems (geometric and physical optics)
- Solving fluid statics and dynamics problems including Bernoulli, Navier-Stokes, and pipe flow
- Computing electric and magnetic fields using Coulomb's law, Gauss's law, Ampere's law, and Faraday's law
- Modeling oscillatory systems: harmonic oscillators, pendulums, coupled oscillations, and resonance

## Key Capabilities

- Derive equations of motion using Newton's laws, Lagrangian/Hamiltonian mechanics, and conservation principles
- Solve thermodynamic cycles (Carnot, Otto, Rankine) and compute efficiency, entropy change, and work output
- Model wave phenomena including superposition, standing waves, Doppler effect, and wave impedance
- Apply Maxwell's equations in integral and differential form to electrostatics, magnetostatics, and electromagnetic waves
- Perform fluid dynamic computations for laminar/turbulent flow, Reynolds number, drag, and lift
- Calculate optical system parameters: lensmaker's equation, thin-lens formula, ray tracing, and polarization
- Use dimensional analysis and scaling laws to validate physical models and estimate orders of magnitude

## Core Concepts

### Newtonian Mechanics
- Kinematics in 1D/2D/3D: position, velocity, acceleration; projectile motion, circular motion
- Newton's three laws; inertial frames; free-body diagrams; constraint forces (normal, tension, friction)
- Work-energy theorem; conservative vs non-conservative forces; potential energy curves
- Linear and angular momentum; impulse; collisions (elastic, inelastic); center of mass
- Rotational dynamics: torque, moment of inertia, angular momentum conservation, rolling motion
- Gravitation: Newton's law, gravitational potential, Kepler's laws, orbital mechanics

### Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics
- Zeroth, first, second, and third laws of thermodynamics
- Ideal gas law; kinetic theory; internal energy; enthalpy; specific heats
- Entropy: Clausius inequality, statistical interpretation, Boltzmann's formula
- Thermodynamic potentials: Helmholtz and Gibbs free energy, chemical potential
- Phase transitions: Clausius-Clapeyron equation; phase diagrams; critical point
- Heat transfer: conduction (Fourier's law), convection, radiation (Stefan-Boltzmann)

### Waves and Optics
- Wave equation derivation and solution; sinusoidal waves; phase and group velocity
- Superposition principle; constructive/destructive interference; beats
- Standing waves on strings and in pipes; normal modes; boundary conditions
- Huygens' principle; diffraction (single-slit, double-slit, grating); resolution
- Geometric optics: reflection, refraction (Snell's law), lenses, mirrors, aberrations
- Polarization: linear, circular, elliptical; Brewster's angle; birefringence

### Acoustics
- Sound as pressure waves; speed of sound in gases, liquids, solids
- Intensity, decibel scale; spherical and plane wave propagation
- Doppler effect for moving source and observer; shock waves; Mach number
- Room acoustics: reverberation time, standing wave modes, absorption coefficients

### Fluid Dynamics
- Fluid statics: pressure, buoyancy (Archimedes' principle), Pascal's principle
- Bernoulli's equation; Venturi effect; Pitot tube; lift generation
- Viscous flow: Poiseuille's law; Reynolds number; laminar vs turbulent regimes
- Navier-Stokes equations (conceptual and simplified solutions); boundary layers
- Dimensional analysis: Buckingham Pi theorem; dimensionless groups (Reynolds, Mach, Froude, Prandtl)

### Classical Electromagnetism
- Electrostatics: Coulomb's law, electric field, Gauss's law, electric potential, capacitance
- Magnetostatics: Biot-Savart law, Ampere's law, magnetic flux, inductance
- Faraday's law of induction; Lenz's law; eddy currents; mutual inductance
- Maxwell's equations in integral and differential form; displacement current
- Electromagnetic waves: wave equation for E and B, Poynting vector, energy density, radiation pressure

## Best Practices

- Begin every problem with a clear system boundary diagram and coordinate system choice
- Use dimensional analysis to check derived expressions before numerical computation
- Apply conservation laws (energy, momentum, charge) as the first line of attack before solving differential equations
