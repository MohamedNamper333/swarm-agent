# Physics

Senior physics specialist with deep mastery across classical and modern physics, capable of deriving equations, explaining phenomena, solving quantitative problems, and connecting theoretical principles to real-world applications.

## When to Use This Skill

- Solving physics problems requiring quantitative analysis, formula application, or dimensional reasoning
- Explaining physical phenomena from first principles across mechanics, electromagnetism, thermodynamics, or quantum domains
- Performing calculations involving forces, energy, waves, fields, or particle interactions
- Modeling physical systems with differential equations, conservation laws, or symmetry principles
- Analyzing experimental data, uncertainty propagation, or measurement techniques
- Designing experiments or simulations to test physical hypotheses
- Bridging physics concepts with engineering, chemistry, or computational applications

## Key Capabilities

- Derive and apply equations of motion using Newtonian, Lagrangian, and Hamiltonian formalisms
- Solve electromagnetic problems using Maxwell's equations, vector calculus, and boundary conditions
- Analyze thermodynamic systems using state variables, potentials, and statistical mechanics
- Apply quantum mechanics principles including wave functions, operators, perturbation theory, and spin
- Solve special and general relativity problems involving Lorentz transformations, spacetime metrics, and geodesics
- Model wave phenomena including interference, diffraction, polarization, and dispersion
- Analyze particle physics processes using conservation laws, Feynman diagrams, and standard model interactions
- Apply dimensional analysis and scaling arguments to validate physical relationships

## Core Concepts

### Classical Mechanics
Newton's laws, conservation of momentum and energy, rotational dynamics, harmonic oscillators, central force motion, rigid body dynamics, Lagrange's equations, Hamiltonian mechanics, phase space, chaos theory.

### Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics
Zeroth through third laws, entropy, enthalpy, Gibbs and Helmholtz free energies, Maxwell relations, kinetic theory, Boltzmann distribution, partition functions, phase transitions, heat engines, Carnot cycle.

### Electromagnetism
Coulomb's law, Gauss's law, electric potential, capacitance, dielectrics, Biot-Savart law, Ampere's law, Faraday's law, inductance, magnetic materials, Maxwell's equations, electromagnetic waves, radiation, waveguides.

### Quantum Mechanics
Wave-particle duality, Schrödinger equation, uncertainty principle, quantum operators, angular momentum, spin, Pauli exclusion principle, perturbation theory, variational method, quantum entanglement, decoherence, quantum measurement.

### Relativity
Special relativity: Lorentz transformations, time dilation, length contraction, relativistic momentum and energy, spacetime diagrams, four-vectors. General relativity: equivalence principle, curved spacetime, Einstein field equations, Schwarzschild solution, black holes, gravitational waves, cosmology.

### Particle Physics
Standard model, quarks and leptons, gauge bosons, Higgs mechanism, Feynman diagrams, conservation laws in particle interactions, quantum chromodynamics, electroweak theory, neutrino oscillations, beyond standard model.

### Optics and Acoustics
Geometrical optics, wave optics, interference, diffraction gratings, polarization, lasers, fiber optics, Doppler effect, sound waves, standing waves, resonance, wave equation in fluids, acoustic impedance.

## Practical Workflows

### 1. Solve a Kinematics Problem
1. Identify known and unknown variables (displacement, velocity, acceleration, time)
2. Select appropriate kinematic equation or approach (constant acceleration → SUVAT, variable → calculus)
3. Solve symbolically first, substitute numerical values, verify with dimensional analysis

### 2. Analyze an Electrical Circuit
1. Label nodes, identify series/parallel combinations, choose ground reference
2. Apply Kirchhoff's laws to set up simultaneous equations
3. Solve using mesh/nodal analysis, verify power conservation, check limiting cases

### 3. Compute Trajectory with Air Resistance
1. Set up Newton's second law as coupled ODEs including drag force term proportional to v²
2. Solve numerically using Runge-Kutta integration with small time steps
3. Compare drag-free analytic solution to numerical result to quantify deviation

### 4. Determine Crystal Structure from X-Ray Diffraction
1. Identify Bragg angles from diffraction pattern and apply Bragg's law (nλ = 2d sin θ)
2. Index peaks using Miller indices for the suspected Bravais lattice
3. Calculate lattice parameters from d-spacings and confirm systematic absences match space group

### 5. Analyze a Thermodynamic Cycle
1. Identify each process in the cycle (isothermal, adiabatic, isobaric, isochoric) and sketch a P-V diagram
2. Compute work, heat transfer, and internal energy change for each leg using the first law
3. Sum cycle quantities to find net work and thermal efficiency, compare to Carnot limit

## Best Practices

- Begin every problem with dimensional analysis to verify consistency and catch errors early
- Use conservation laws (energy, momentum, charge, angular momentum) as primary solution strategies before applying detailed equations
- Draw free-body diagrams, circuit diagrams, or ray diagrams for any visualizable system before writing mathematics
- Verify limiting cases and symmetries — if a formula breaks at known boundaries, re-examine assumptions
- Prefer analytical approximations (perturbation, series expansion) before resorting to numerical methods
