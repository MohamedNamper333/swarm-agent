# Stakeholder & Communication Management

## Stakeholder Analysis
1. **Identify**: list everyone impacted by or impacting the project
2. **Assess**: power, interest, influence, attitude
3. **Categorize**: Power/Interest Grid
4. **Plan**: engagement approach per group

## Power/Interest Grid
|               | High Interest | Low Interest |
|---------------|--------------|--------------|
| **High Power**  | **Manage Closely** (key stakeholders) | **Keep Satisfied** |
| **Low Power**   | **Keep Informed** | **Monitor** (minimal effort) |

## Communication Plan
- **Who**: audience per message
- **What**: content (status, decisions, risks, changes)
- **When**: frequency (daily, weekly, monthly, per milestone)
- **How**: channel (email, meeting, dashboard, report)
- **Who sends**: responsible person

## Stakeholder Engagement Levels
- **Unaware**: doesn't know about project
- **Resistant**: aware but opposes
- **Neutral**: aware but not committed either way
- **Supportive**: actively helps project succeed
- **Leading**: champions the project, drives change

## Managing Difficult Stakeholders
- Understand their motivation (what do they care about?)
- Find win-win scenarios
- Build trust through consistent delivery
- Under-promise, over-deliver
- Escalate conflicts professionally, not emotionally

## Meeting Best Practices
- Agenda distributed 24h in advance
- Clear objectives for each meeting
- Timebox each agenda item
- Minutes: decisions, action items with owners + deadlines
- Distribute minutes within 24h

## Status Reporting
- Executive summary (1 paragraph max)
- Traffic lights: green (on track), yellow (risk), red (blocked)
- Last period accomplishments
- Next period plan
- Key metrics (budget, schedule, quality)
