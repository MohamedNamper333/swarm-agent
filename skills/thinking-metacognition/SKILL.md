# Metacognition & Learning

## Metacognitive Cycle
1. **Plan**: What am I trying to achieve? What do I already know?
2. **Monitor**: Am I understanding? Is this approach working?
3. **Evaluate**: Did I succeed? What would I do differently?

## Cognitive Biases to Watch
- **Dunning-Kruger**: we overestimate our ability in unfamiliar domains
- **Confirmation bias**: we seek information that confirms our beliefs
- **Halo effect**: one positive trait colors our entire perception
- **Sunk cost**: we continue because we've already invested
- **Overconfidence**: we're more confident than accurate

## Effective Learning Strategies
- **Active recall**: test yourself instead of re-reading
- **Spaced repetition**: review at increasing intervals
- **Interleaving**: mix different topics in one session
- **Elaboration**: explain concepts in your own words
- **Dual coding**: combine text + visuals
- **Concrete examples**: abstract concept → real example

## Feynman Technique
1. Choose a concept
2. Teach it to a child (simple language, no jargon)
3. Identify gaps — where did you struggle to explain?
4. Review and simplify — fill the gaps, then repeat

## Reflection Framework
- What went well? Why?
- What went wrong? Why?
- What will I do differently next time?
- What did I learn about myself/my process?

## Cognitive Load Management
- Chunk complex information into groups
- Eliminate extraneous information
- Build mental models — connect new info to existing frameworks
- Automate routine thinking to free mental capacity
