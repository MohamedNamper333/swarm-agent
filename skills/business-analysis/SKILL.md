# Business & Competitive Analysis

## Strategic Frameworks

### SWOT Analysis
| | Positive | Negative |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal** | Strengths | Weaknesses |
| **External** | Opportunities | Threats |

- Strengths: what gives you advantage? Resources, capabilities, IP, talent
- Weaknesses: where are you vulnerable? Gaps, dependencies, cost structure
- Opportunities: market trends, unmet needs, emerging tech, regulatory shifts
- Threats: competitors, substitutes, regulation changes, supply chain risks

### PESTLE Analysis
- **P**olitical: government policy, stability, trade restrictions, tax policy
- **E**conomic: growth, inflation, interest rates, unemployment, disposable income
- **S**ocial: demographics, cultural trends, lifestyle changes, education
- **T**echnological: innovation, automation, R&D, tech infrastructure
- **L**egal: regulations, labor laws, IP protection, consumer protection
- **E**nvironmental: climate impact, sustainability, resource scarcity, regulations

### Porter's Five Forces
1. **Competitive Rivalry**: number of competitors, industry growth, differentiation
2. **Threat of New Entrants**: barriers to entry, capital requirements, economies of scale
3. **Threat of Substitutes**: alternative solutions, switching costs, price-performance trade-off
4. **Bargaining Power of Buyers**: concentration, price sensitivity, switching costs
5. **Bargaining Power of Suppliers**: concentration, uniqueness of inputs, switching costs

## Market Analysis
- **TAM/SAM/SOM**: Total Addressable → Serviceable Available → Serviceable Obtainable
- Top-down: industry reports × % segments
- Bottom-up: unit price × #customers (more reliable)
- Growth rate: CAGR, YoY trends, market maturity stage
- Competitive positioning: BCG matrix (Stars, Cash Cows, Question Marks, Dogs)

## OKRs (Objectives & Key Results)
- **Objective**: inspirational, qualitative, time-bound (e.g., "Dominate the premium segment")
- **Key Results**: 3-5 measurable outcomes (e.g., "30% market share by Q4")
- Each KR must have: baseline, target, measurement method
- Leading indicators (predict future success) vs lagging (measure past success)

## Business Model Canvas
- Value Proposition, Customer Segments, Channels, Customer Relationships
- Revenue Streams, Key Resources, Key Activities, Key Partnerships, Cost Structure

## Analysis Output
- Data-backed claims (cite sources, note confidence)
- Separate facts from inferences from recommendations
- Identify what's unknown and how to find out
- Quantify where possible: market size $, growth %, timeline
