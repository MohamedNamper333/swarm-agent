# Decision Making Frameworks

## RAPID Framework
- **R**ecommend: who proposes the decision?
- **A**gree: who must approve?
- **P**erform: who executes once decided?
- **I**nput: who provides data/opinions?
- **D**ecide: who makes the final call?

## Decision Matrix (Weighted)
1. List options (rows) and criteria (columns)
2. Weight each criterion by importance (1-10)
3. Score each option per criterion (1-10)
4. Multiply score × weight, sum per option
5. Highest score = best choice

## Eisenhower Matrix
|               | Urgent | Not Urgent |
|---------------|--------|------------|
| **Important** | Do Now | Schedule   |
| **Not Imp.**  | Delegate | Delete |

## Cost-Benefit Analysis
- Quantify all costs (money, time, opportunity, risk)
- Quantify all benefits (revenue, efficiency, satisfaction)
- Calculate ROI, payback period, net present value
- Consider intangible factors too (culture, brand, morale)

## Prospect Theory
- Losses feel ~2× more painful than equivalent gains
- Frame decisions accordingly

## Pre-Mortem
- Assume the decision failed spectacularly
- Work backwards: what caused the failure?
- Identify and mitigate those risks proactively

## OODA Loop (Observe-Orient-Decide-Act)
- **Observe**: gather data
- **Orient**: analyze and contextualize
- **Decide**: choose action
- **Act**: execute and collect feedback (loop back)
